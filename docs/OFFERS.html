Pulse — the wellness & risk copilot

You picked all four lenses. They read the tracking and activity Doorline already captures — no new hardware, no extra data collection — and turn it into people signals, not just sales numbers.

💚 Wellbeing — support-first

Hours-on, streaks without a break, and slumps become gentle prompts: a rep self check-in, and a private “worth checking on Jordan” nudge to the manager.

Serves: the rep first, then the manager.

🛟 Safety — lone-worker

No check-in for X hours, off-route far from any assigned door, or a panic tap raises a manager alert. Field staff working alone get a safety net.

Serves: manager / office, in real time.

📉 Attrition & performance risk

Falling activity, dropping close rate, or fading logins flag a rep trending toward a slump or the door — early enough to coach, not exit-interview.

Serves: manager + Pivot coaches.

🛡️ Compliance risk

Worked outside assigned territory, missing tracking consent, or hours pushing labor limits get flagged before they become a liability.

Serves: the company / admin.
The trust line this has to hold.

Human-centered means consent-based and support-framed. Reps opt in (Doorline already gates tracking on consent), wellbeing signals are shown to the rep themselves, and the language is “here to help,” never “gotcha.” Get this framing right and Pulse is a retention feature; get it wrong and it reads as spyware. It's the single most important design decision in this tier.

The White-Label House

The scalable model: every company is a branded, isolated house on one shared platform — not a separate app to maintain — with Pivot as the landlord who sees the whole estate. Doorline already isolates data per company; this adds the branding depth and the builder.

  • Their logo, name, and colors throughout — plus their own domain.
  • Their own reps, campaigns, contracts, and Pivot-built training — walled off from every other house.
  • Pivot parent dashboard: every house's health, seats, and Pulse flags in one overseer view.
  • One codebase, one deploy — so it scales to many houses without multiplying what can break.

How training gets built

Per your call: it's a service, not a self-serve library. The platform is the delivery vehicle; Pivot is the content engine.

1 · Company chooses topics

In the House Builder they check the skills they want — door approach, objections, closing, product-specific pitches — or Pivot recommends a set from a discovery conversation.

2 · Pivot builds it for a fee

Pivot produces the lessons/scripts and loads them into that company's house. Priced per course or as a build package — high-margin services on top of the seat license.

3 · It lands where reps work

Lessons appear on My Day; scripts surface on the Street Sheet triggered by the rep's own dispositions. Certification badges show on the Leaderboard.

4 · Pulse closes the loop

Pulse and the accountability data tell Pivot which reps need which coaching next — so the training keeps targeting the real gap instead of a generic curriculum.

Keep the "managers are free" lever.

Across every tier, bill only field reps. A 10-person crew with 3 managers pays for 7 — a structural 30% cost advantage no per-seat competitor can match without changing their billing model. That's the headline, not the sticker price.
